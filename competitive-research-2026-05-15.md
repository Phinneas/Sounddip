# Sounddip Competitive Research
May 2026

Sounddip is a directory for sound bath practitioners and experiences. This document tracks what we've learned about the competitive landscape and what to do with it.

---

## The Opportunity in One Sentence

A single blog post from an LA hypnotherapy practice ranks #3 on Google for "sound bath Los Angeles" — a purpose-built directory should own this space with minimal resistance.

---

## Who We're Up Against

**The two closest structural competitors are retreat.guru and bookretreats.com** — both are booking/directory platforms for wellness retreats. retreat.guru has about 9,000 keywords and solid backlink authority from established meditation and retreat centers. bookretreats.com is bigger (21K keywords, ~150K monthly visitors) and is probably the benchmark to beat long-term.

**mindbodyonline.com** is broader — it's a booking platform for yoga studios, gyms, and wellness businesses generally. Lots of keywords (149K) but their sound bath coverage is almost nonexistent and their backlink profile is surprisingly weak (most linking domains are tiny, low-authority studios).

**Eventbrite** is a nuisance competitor for local queries. It shows up for "sound bath [city]" event searches and is hard to displace for that specific intent. We should target the discovery/directory angle ("best sound baths in [city]") rather than competing directly with event listings.

**The editorial sites** (Timeout, Travel + Leisure, Condé Nast Traveler, Discover LA, Secret LA) aren't competitors — they're link targets. They write "best X in [city]" roundups and none of them have meaningful sound bath coverage yet.

---

## What the Keywords Tell Us

The sound bath keyword universe is small but it's ours to take. The core terms:

- *sound bath los angeles* — 880 searches/month, modernhypno.com ranks #3 with a single blog post
- *sound bath studio near me* — 880/month, nobody strong ranking
- *floating sound bath* — 1,900/month, weak competition
- *floating sound bath near me* — 1,900/month, wide open

The pattern across all of these is the same: whoever wrote a "best sound baths in [city]" roundup page is ranking, because nobody built a proper directory. That's the gap.

---

## What the Backlink Profiles Tell Us

We pulled full backlink profiles for retreat.guru and mindbodyonline.com from SE Ranking.

**retreat.guru** has 319 referring domains and nearly all their links come from retreat centers and meditation organizations that list their programs on the platform. Their top linking sites — Esalen Institute, Spirit Rock Meditation Center, Art of Living, Hollyhock — are exactly the kind of high-authority wellness institutions Sounddip should target. The link model is simple: practitioners list on the directory, then link back from their own website. Sound bath content makes up less than 0.5% of retreat.guru's backlinks — the niche is essentially unclaimed.

**mindbodyonline.com** has 1,031 referring domains but the quality is poor — 44% of their linking domains have a trust score below 20. Their links come from tiny yoga studios and gyms that use their booking software. Same model, lower authority. Sound healing is only 1.1% of their link profile.

**The takeaway:** Neither competitor has done any real link building around sound baths. The space is wide open editorially.

---

## How to Build Links (What Actually Works Here)

Both competitors built their backlink profiles the same way — the businesses they serve link back from their own websites. For Sounddip that means:

**Step 1 — Practitioner badge program.** When a sound bath practitioner gets listed on Sounddip, give them a badge or booking button to embed on their site. Each listing becomes a referring domain. This is exactly how retreat.guru got links from Spirit Rock and Esalen.

**Step 2 — Go after the high-authority wellness institutions.** Esalen (DT82), Spirit Rock (DT80), Art of Living (DT76), and Hollyhock (DT73) are already linking to retreat.guru. If Sounddip has relevant listings, there's a reason to pitch them.

**Step 3 — Editorial placements.** City guide sites like Timeout, Secret LA, Discover LA, and The Infatuation write "best X in [city]" content constantly. None of them have a solid sound bath piece yet. Once we have city pages live, these are obvious outreach targets.
