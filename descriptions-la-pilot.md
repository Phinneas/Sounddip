## 1. Sound Bath Los Angeles / One Conduit (Carlina Baldonado Sullivan)

**Bio (practitioner page):**
Carlina Baldonado Sullivan runs One Conduit out of Hollywood, though the practice rarely stays in one room. She'll set up a private session in a Beverly Hills living room, lead a corporate group in Santa Monica, or drive up the coast to Santa Barbara for a retreat — the gongs and crystal bowls travel with her. Her style favors attention over performance: less about producing a specific effect, more about reading what a room and a group need that day. Sessions run as private one-on-ones, small groups, or full corporate bookings, with format adjusted for each. If you've sat through a sound bath that felt scripted, this is the corrective — present and responsive rather than rehearsed.

**Session description:**
Crystal bowls + gong · 60–90 min · Hollywood, with sessions across Santa Monica, Beverly Hills, and Pasadena by request. Private, corporate, and small-group formats each run differently — nothing here is one-size-fits-all. Book online.

## 5. The Soundbath Center (Jamie Bechtold)

**Bio (practitioner page):**
Jamie Bechtold has been running sound baths in Los Angeles since 2004 and has led more than 4,000 public sessions out of The Soundbath Center in Eagle Rock since it opened in 2015. That's not a typo — it's the kind of volume that makes this one of the most established sound healing institutions in the city, with a gong-forward style and its own practitioner certification program for people who want to learn the craft rather than just attend. Worth knowing before you book: reviews are more polarized here than at smaller studios, which tracks with running thousands of sessions for thousands of different people rather than a small, hand-picked group.

**Session description:**
Gong-focused sound bath · Not published · The Soundbath Center, Eagle Rock. Also runs mobile sessions (7 gongs, 10 crystal bowls) and practitioner certification training. One of the longest-running sound bath operations in LA — book through the studio's own calendar.

## 6. Well & Whole (Gaia)

**Bio (practitioner page):**
Gaia runs Well & Whole out of the Westchester/Playa area, where yoga therapy, sound healing, and Reiki are offered as a connected practice rather than separate menu items. What sets this listing apart is pricing transparency — classes start at $35, private sessions at $124, and full-day retreats at $244, numbers most LA practitioners don't publish. The sessions lean therapeutic rather than ceremonial: less incense-and-intention, more structured bodywork with sound as one tool among several. A solid option for someone who wants the benefits without the spiritual framing turned all the way up.

**Session description:**
Yoga therapy + sound healing + Reiki · Varies by format · Westchester / Playa area. Published pricing across every tier — classes from $35, private sessions from $124, day retreats from $244. Book online.

## 7. The Sound Healing Studio

**Bio (practitioner page):**
The Sound Healing Studio in Koreatown runs on three tracks at once: a weekly public sound bath in what they call the Gong Room, a retail counter selling alchemy crystal singing bowls (the good ones start around $1,000), and a certification program for people training to become sound healers themselves. It's less a single practitioner's listing and more a small institution — useful to know going in, since the energy is closer to a working studio than an intimate one-on-one space. If you're sound-bath-curious and might want to learn the craft eventually, this is one of the few LA spaces set up for that whole arc.

**Session description:**
Gong Room sound bath · Not published · Koreatown. Weekly public sessions; also home to a singing bowl retail counter and sound healer certification training. Book through the studio's own site.

Crystal bowls, frame drums, handpan, chimes · Not published · Pasadena. Private sessions for 1–10 people, plus a standing weekly group sound bath. Contact the studio directly for current pricing.
